Storyline

Major is a biographical war drama that chronicles the life of Major Sandeep Unnikrishnan, an officer in the National Security Guard (NSG) who sacrificed his life during the 2008 Mumbai attacks. The film explores his journey from a young cadet to becoming a decorated commando, highlighting his dedication, training, and ultimate heroism.

The story focuses on the 26/11 terror attacks on Mumbai, where Major Unnikrishnan played a crucial role in combating the terrorists. His actions during the siege at the Taj Mahal Palace Hotel became legendary, as he single-handedly took down multiple terrorists while protecting civilians.

Plot Summary

The film opens with the intense sequence of the 2008 Mumbai attacks and then flashes back to show Major Sandeep Unnikrishnan's early life, his training at the National Defence Academy, and his rigorous preparation to join the elite NSG commandos. It portrays his personal life, relationships, and the challenges he faced in his professional career.

As the narrative builds up to the fateful night of November 26, 2008, the film captures the chaos, bravery, and sacrifice that defined those terrifying hours. Major Unnikrishnan's story serves as an inspiration and a tribute to all the security personnel who risk their lives to protect the nation.

Related Information

Historical Context

The 2008 Mumbai attacks, also known as 26/11, were a series of terrorist attacks that lasted four days, killing 166 people and injuring over 300 others.

Major Sandeep Unnikrishnan

Ashoka Chakra recipient who served in the Indian Army and NSG. He was posthumously awarded India's highest peacetime gallantry award.

NSG Operations

The National Security Guard is India's elite counter-terrorism force, trained to handle hostage situations and terrorist attacks.

Critical Reception

The film received generally positive reviews for Adivi Sesh's performance and its patriotic theme, though some criticized its dramatization of real events.
